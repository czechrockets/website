The rocket shares similarities with those launched in the Czech Rocket Challenge, but CRS members build all its components from scratch, including the engine.

Stopa≈ô's main mission was to unite the newly formed CRS team and see if they could build a rocket capable of flying from scratch. Inspired by SpaceX founder Elon Musk, who put his Tesla Roadster on his Falcon Heavy rocket, CRS chose a miniature car as Stopa≈ô's payload. The rocket released the car at the highest altitude with a parachute and filmed its journey back to Earth using a mini camera.

The rocket is a 1.2-meter-tall, single-stage rocket with a 60 mm diameter composite fuselage provided by CompoTech, propelled by solid fuel (a mixture of sorbitol and potassium nitrate). It can reach an apogee of up to 500 meters and land safely with a parachute. During its 70-second-long flight, Stopa≈ô reaches speeds of up to 55 m/s, similar to the maximum speed of a Cessna 152 airplane.
